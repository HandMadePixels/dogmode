DogMode in the Enterprise
Reprinted with permission of The Enterprise, Utah's business journal, www.slenterprise.com.

By Barbara Rattle
The Enterprise

DogMode, a nine-year-old Murray business that offers canine boarding and “doggie day care,” has greatly expanded the size and scope of its operations.

Formerly located in approximately 6,000 square feet at 4010 S. 210 W., the firm moved about 10 days ago into a 15,000 square foot facility on one acre at 4030 S. 210 W. that also includes an indoor/outdoor area for its resident pooches.

“Our other facility was very good and we had a great following and we grew, but this new facility is bigger and better. We’ve purchased state-of-the-art materials like drain systems and flush lines,” said owner Jon Campbell. “We’re the first facility of our kind in the state and have won Best of State awards in 2003 and 2004 and a City Weekly Best of Utah award in 2001.”

Campbell said the new DogMode facility features 100 kennels — with elevated therapeutic beds — in addition to a large indoor/outdoor play area where dogs are able to choose between being outside surrounded by a cinderblock wall (to prevent digging or jumping) or being inside in a temperature-controlled environment. DogMode is open 365 days a year and its staff, which is currently being increased, continuously monitors the facility’s canine denizens, “giving them plenty of love and attention,” Campbell said.

At night, the facility is unmanned, but Campbell said the structure is highly safe and secure, equipped with carbon monoxide and smoke detectors, burglar alarms and motion sensors.

Campbell said he is in the process of setting up “doggie Web cams” so pet owners will be able to keep an eye on their pooches via a new DogMode Web site that is nearing completion.

“It’s a much safer and more secure environment than just leaving your dogs at home during the day while you go to work,” he said.

All dogs that patronize DogMode are subject to three requirements — that they are fully vaccinated, that they are spayed or neutered if older than seven months and that they get along well with other canines.

“We have a separate room that’s solely for the purpose of screening. We call it the ‘sniffing room,’” Campbell said. “We conduct what we call a doggie interview. We take them in the room without the owner to see how they behave. We take them off leash, then bring in a male dog, a female dog, an old dog, a young dog, a big dog, a little dog, a rowdy dog and a mellow dog just to see what their behavior is. If that goes all right, we schedule a day of day care for you, free.”

DogMode also offers dog-bathing services. Clients can either bathe their dogs themselves or have DogMode staff do so.

Despite having a much larger and better facility, Campbell said his prices have remained largely unchanged. Day care pricing remains the same, at $20 for the first day and $15 for each additional day of the same week. Boarding prices have been slightly adjusted. While a sliding scale based on the number of overnight stays was formerly employed, Campbell said he opted to eliminate any possible confusion on the part of customers and charge a flat $30 per day.

In November 2004, DogMode published a book titled Dog Friendly Parks and Trails in the Wasatch. Fifty percent of the profits from the book’s sale are donated to Intermountain Therapy Animals. DogMode is also supports the No More Homeless Pets program by serving as a foster home at no charge for the dogs of Wasatch Humane, Humane Society of Utah, Best Friends, Friends of Utah Bassets and others.

DogMode will host a “pet adoptathon” in conjunction with Humane Society of Utah April 1-2.
